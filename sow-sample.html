Statement of Work

Engagement: Human + AI Operating System — Discovery, Solution Design & Pilot
Provider: Rustic Agentic (Scott Baker)  ·  Client: [Client] (regional specialty-services firm, anonymized)  ·  Date: [DATE]  ·  SOW #: [001]

1 · Objective & Background

ProblemThe operations team spends ~30% of capacity on repetitive triage, lookup, and drafting across email, a CRM, and document stores. Knowledge lives in people's heads; turnaround and decision quality vary by who is on shift.
ObjectiveDesign and pilot a governed Human + AI operating model that cuts time-to-response and standardizes decision quality — without sacrificing security, compliance, or human accountability.
Baseline to confirmDuring discovery we will measure today's volume and time-per-step (e.g., N requests/day × M minutes each) so the pilot's results are provable against a real number, not a guess.

2 · Scope of Work

In scope
  • Current-state workflow mapping & bottleneck analysis
  • Prioritized opportunity list (value × feasibility)
  • Solution blueprint + governance plan
  • Requirements with acceptance criteria
  • One pilot workflow, human-in-the-loop, measured vs. baseline
Out of scope (this SOW)
  • Production rollout beyond the single pilot workflow
  • Net-new integrations not named in requirements
  • Data migration / cleansing of source systems
  • 24×7 operations or a managed care plan (separate SOW)
  • Model fine-tuning / custom model training

3 · Deliverables

D1 — Current-state mapDocumented as-is workflow, handoffs, swivel-chair steps, and the quantified bottleneck (volume + time).
D2 — Opportunity listPrioritized automation opportunities scored by value and feasibility; the recommended pilot.
D3 — Solution blueprintFuture-state Human + AI operating model: what AI handles vs. where a human stays in the loop, plus the reference architecture.
D4 — Governance planAccess model (scoped/revocable), data handling/residency, audit/logging, and approval gates by risk class.
D5 — RequirementsFunctional requirements each with an explicit acceptance criterion (the spec is the eval).
D6 — Pilot + readoutOne workflow built and run with a human in the loop; measured against D1 baseline with a go/no-go recommendation.

4 · Approach & Timeline

Phase 1 — Discovery & Design ~1–2 weeksD1–D5. Stakeholder interviews, current-state map, blueprint, governance plan, requirements. Gate: signed-off requirements before any build.
Phase 2 — Pilot ~2–4 weeksD6. Build one high-value workflow, human-in-the-loop, measured against baseline. Gate: go/no-go on the agreed success metrics.
Phase 3 — Harden & Expand future SOWAdd guardrails/evals, codify reusable methods, extend to adjacent workflows. Scoped separately once the pilot proves out.

5 · Acceptance Criteria

  • Each deliverable is accepted when it meets the criteria defined in D5 and is reviewed with the Client sponsor.
  • Pilot success (illustrative, set with Client in Phase 1): ↓ median response time ≥ 40% on the pilot workflow · 100% of AI-assisted decisions logged & reviewable · zero unapproved data egress.
  • Client has 5 business days to review each deliverable; silence past that = accepted.

7 · Access, Security & Assumptions

  • Scoped, revocable access only. Client provisions a least-privilege, Client-owned service account / IAM user the Client can revoke at any time — never a root/owner login. Full scope is agreed in writing before work begins.
  • Data stays in the Client's perimeter (their cloud account/VPC); no proprietary data trains a public model; PII/PHI handling and residency defined in D4.
  • Human accountability: AI never finalizes a high-stakes or customer-facing action — approval gates by risk class.
  • Assumptions: Client provides timely access to named stakeholders, sample data, and the systems in scope. Delays in access shift the timeline, not the scope.
