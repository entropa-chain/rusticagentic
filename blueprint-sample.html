Solution Blueprint: Human + AI Operating System

Client: regional specialty-services firm (anonymized) · Engagement: discovery → solution design → implementation plan

1 · Problem & Objective

ProblemOperations team spends ~30% of capacity on repetitive triage, drafting, and lookup across email, a CRM, and document stores. Knowledge lives in people's heads; decision quality and turnaround vary by who's on shift.
ObjectiveReduce time-to-response and standardize decision quality with a Human + AI operating model — without sacrificing governance, security, or human accountability.
Success metrics↓ median response time 50% · ↓ rework 30% · 100% of AI-assisted decisions logged & reviewable · zero unapproved data egress.

2 · Current-State Workflow (as-is)

Inbound request ─▶ Human triage (manual) ─▶ Lookup across CRM + docs (manual) ─▶ Draft response (manual) └─▶ escalate (ad hoc, undocumented) ─▶ Decision (varies by individual)
  • Bottlenecks: manual lookup, inconsistent triage, undocumented escalation logic.
  • Risk: institutional knowledge not captured; quality depends on the individual.

3 · Future-State Human + AI Operating Model

Inbound ─▶ AI triage + classify ─▶ AI retrieves context (RAG over governed knowledge base) ─▶ AI drafts response + recommended action ─▶ HUMAN review / approve (in the loop) ─▶ Send └─▶ every decision + rationale captured back into the knowledge base (institutional learning)

AI handles

  • Classification & triage
  • Context retrieval (RAG)
  • First-draft responses & SOP lookups

Human stays in the loop

  • Approval on anything customer-facing or high-stakes
  • Exceptions & judgment calls
  • Final accountability

4 · Solution Architecture (components)

OrchestrationAgentic workflow layer routing tasks between LLM steps, tools, and human approval gates.
ModelsLLM (Claude / GPT) for reasoning & drafting; smaller model for classification to control cost.
Knowledge layerVector database + RAG over a governed, access-controlled corpus (SOPs, prior decisions).
IntegrationsAPI connectors to CRM, email, and document store (least-privilege service accounts).
Guardrails / evalOutput validation, human-approval gates on defined risk classes, and an evaluation harness measuring quality before rollout.
Audit & memoryEvery AI action + human decision logged; approved outcomes feed back as institutional knowledge.

5 · Data Governance & Security

  • Access: least-privilege, scoped, revocable service accounts per integration; no broad/owner credentials.
  • Data handling: PII/PHI classified and masked before model calls where not required; data residency & retention defined up front.
  • Human accountability: AI never finalizes a high-stakes action — approval gates by risk class.
  • Auditability: full decision log (prompt, retrieval, output, approver) for review & compliance.

6 · Implementation Plan (phased)

Phase 1 — Discovery & design (1–2 wks)Current-state map, prioritized opportunities, this blueprint, success metrics, governance plan.
Phase 2 — Pilot (2–4 wks)One high-value workflow, human-in-the-loop, measured against baseline. Go/no-go on metrics.
Phase 3 — Harden & expand (ongoing)Add guardrails/evals, codify reusable methods, extend to adjacent workflows, capture institutional knowledge.

7 · Risks & Mitigations

"Looks-right-but-wrong" AI outputEvaluation harness + human approval gates before any customer-facing action.
Data exposureScoped access, PII/PHI masking, audit logging, defined residency/retention.
Low adoptionDesign with the operators; AI augments rather than replaces; measure and show wins from the pilot.

8 · Delivery Method — Agentic Engineering

I build AI systems the way reliable software is built, not the way demos are thrown together. The guiding principle is Agent = Model + Harness: reliability comes from the structure around the model, not the model alone.

  • Outcome first. Define the measurable outcome and its acceptance test before building — the spec is the eval, not an afterthought.
  • Verification covers the hard 20%. AI produces ~80% of a solution fast; the edge cases and "looks-right-but-wrong" failures are caught by tests, evals, and human-approval gates — not by generating faster.
  • Guardrails, skills & memory. Non-negotiable rules, reusable methodologies the system loads on demand, and persistent institutional knowledge — so quality is repeatable and compounds over time.
  • Governance throughout. Scoped access, audit, and human-in-the-loop are designed in from the start, never bolted on.
  • Every failure becomes a permanent guardrail or test — a problem is fixed once and caught forever, which is what turns an AI pilot into a dependable operating system.
